    Algebra review
    ðŸ”—
    The final part of this math review is a brief review of algebra. We use algebra to rearrange mathematical expressions to solve for some unknown. There are two things to remember when doing algebraic rearrangements. First, our goal is to isolate the variable of interest, remembering that it must be in the numerator if weâ€™re dealing with fractions. Second, the way in which we will isolate the variable is by carrying out opposite operations on both sides of the equation. Some useful reminders:
    ðŸ”—
    1. Addition and subtraction are opposite operations; multiplication and division are opposite operations
      ðŸ”—
      1. Example: \(32=x-3\)
        ðŸ”—
        To isolate x we must add three to both sides because addition is the opposite operation for subtraction
        ðŸ”—
        \(32=x-3 \rightarrow\)
        ðŸ”—
        \(32+3=x-3+3 \rightarrow\)
        ðŸ”—
        \(35=x\)
        ðŸ”—
        ðŸ”—
        ðŸ”—
      2. Example: \(32=\frac{x}{2}\)
        ðŸ”—
        To isolate x we must multiply both sides by 2 because multiplication is the opposite operation for division
        ðŸ”—
        \(32=\frac{x}{2} \rightarrow\)
        ðŸ”—
        \(32\times 2=\frac{x}{2}\times 2 \rightarrow\)
        ðŸ”—
        \(64=x\)
        ðŸ”—
        ðŸ”—
        ðŸ”—
      ðŸ”—
    2. If there are multiple quantities that must be moved, work in the direction of the reverse of the order of operations
      ðŸ”—
      1. Example: \(3x+5=14\)
        ðŸ”—
        We have to use two reverse operations (subtraction and division) to isolate x, but which do we do first? If we were computing the left side of the equation, weâ€™d first multiply 3 times x and then add 5. When isolating x, we must go in the reverse order of operations. So in this case we need to undo the addition of 5 before undoing the multiplication by 3. So the first thing we do is subtract 5 from both sides. Then we can divide by 3.
        ðŸ”—
        \(3x+5=14 \rightarrow\)
        ðŸ”—
        \(3x+5-5=14-5 \rightarrow\)
        ðŸ”—
        \(3x=9 \rightarrow\)
        ðŸ”—
        \(3x\div3=9\div3 \rightarrow\)
        ðŸ”—
        \(x=3\)
        ðŸ”—
        ðŸ”—
        ðŸ”—
      2. Example: \(\frac{x+5}{2}=7\)
        ðŸ”—
        If we were computing the left side of the equation, weâ€™d first add 5 to x and then divide the sum by 2. The reverse order would have us first multiply both sides of the equation by two and then subtract 5 from both sides.
        ðŸ”—
        \(\frac{x+5}{2}=7 \rightarrow\)
        ðŸ”—
        \(\frac{x+5}{2}\times2=7\times2 \rightarrow\)
        ðŸ”—
        \(x+5=14 \rightarrow\)
        ðŸ”—
        \(x+5-5=14-5 \rightarrow\)
        ðŸ”—
        \(x=9\)
        ðŸ”—
        ðŸ”—
        ðŸ”—
      ðŸ”—
    3. If the unknown is in the denominator of a fraction, you need to move it to the numerator (NOTE: Everything else should first be moved to the other side other than the fraction with your unknown). You can do this by either multiplying both sides by the unknown or by taking the reciprocal (ie. flipping) of both sides of the equation.
      ðŸ”—
      1. Example (Method 1): solve for x in the following equation \(\frac{2}{x}=\frac{4}{a}\)
        ðŸ”—
        Multiply both sides of the equation by x to get it in the numerator. Then move the 4 and the "a" to the other side of the equation by dividing and multiplying, respectively.
        ðŸ”—
        \(\frac{2}{x}\times x=\frac{4}{a}\times x\rightarrow\)
        ðŸ”—
        \(2=\frac{4}{a}\times x\rightarrow\)
        ðŸ”—
        \(2\times a=\frac{4}{a}\times x\times a\rightarrow\)
        ðŸ”—
        \(2\times a=4\times x\rightarrow\)
        ðŸ”—
        \(2\times a\div 4=4\times x \div 4\rightarrow\)
        ðŸ”—
        \(\frac{a}{2}=x\)
        ðŸ”—
        ðŸ”—
        ðŸ”—
      2. Example (Method 2): solve for x in the following equation \(\frac{2}{x}=\frac{4}{a}\)
        ðŸ”—
        Take the reciprocal of both sides of the equation (ie. flip the fractions on both sides). Then move the 2 to the other side of the equation by multiplying both sides by 2.
        ðŸ”—
        \(\frac{2}{x}=\frac{4}{a}\rightarrow\)
        ðŸ”—
        \(\frac{x}{2}=\frac{a}{4}\rightarrow\)
        ðŸ”—
        \(\frac{x}{2}\times 2=\frac{a}{4}\times 2\rightarrow\)
        ðŸ”—
        \(x=\frac{a}{2}\)
        ðŸ”—
        ðŸ”—
